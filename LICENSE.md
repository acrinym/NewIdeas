## Why GPL v3?

Cycloside is a **resurrection project**—we're reclaiming what corporations killed (WebTV, Winamp, shellcity.net, desktop customization culture). To ensure Cycloside cannot be captured, closed, or killed again, we use **strong copyleft**:

- **You can use Cycloside freely** (personal, commercial, any purpose)
- **You can modify Cycloside** (fork, improve, customize)
- **You can distribute Cycloside** (share binaries, host your own marketplace)
- **But:** If you distribute modified versions, you **must** share the source code under GPL v3

This prevents:
- Corporations from buying and closing Cycloside
- Proprietary forks that fragment the community
- Platform owners from "embracing and extinguishing" the project

**Cycloside is a commons. It belongs to everyone. It cannot be enclosed.**

## Plugin and Theme Licensing

**Plugins, themes, and packs you create are YOURS.**

You are **not required** to license your plugins/themes under GPL. You may use:
- GPL v3 (if you want your plugin to be copyleft)
- MIT, Apache, BSD (if you want permissive licensing)
- Proprietary/commercial (if you want to sell your plugin)

The **Cycloside SDK and API** are GPL v3, but your plugins are considered **separate works** that use the API. You control your plugin's license.

**Exception:** If you modify Cycloside's core code (not just writing a plugin), those modifications **must** be GPL v3.

## Commercial Use

**Yes, you can use Cycloside commercially:**
- Sell plugins/themes/packs you create
- Use Cycloside in your business
- Offer Cycloside customization services
- Bundle Cycloside with your products

**You cannot:**
- Sell Cycloside itself (it's free software)
- Distribute modified Cycloside without providing source code
- Prevent users from redistributing your modified version

## AGPL? Why Not?

We considered AGPL (which extends GPL to network services), but:
- Cycloside is primarily a desktop app (not a network service)
- AGPL would complicate plugin licensing
- GPL v3 is sufficient for our threat model

If someone tries to run "Cycloside as a Service" (SaaS) without sharing source, the community can pressure them or fork the project.
